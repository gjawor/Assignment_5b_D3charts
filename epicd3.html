Even though the Boston harbor has existed and been in use for a long time, no one was ever cleaning it. The pollution buildup caused extreme damage to the waterways and local ecosystem. In 1972 the Clean Water Act passed which helped motivate a cleanup project for the harbor. While the cost to clean up these polluted spaces is very expensive, estimated at 4.7 billion dollars, the return and benefit from the ecosystem justifies the cost. This study explores monetary values for the ecosystem, as well as the impact that the clean up has had so far. Restoring and maintaining the local ecosystem is important for the many organisms its home to, and also helps raise the value of the land which is between 30 - 100 billion.
